Beef Bistek Tagalog

Dish Description:

Beef Bistek Tagalog is a classic Filipino dish made with thinly sliced beef marinated and simmered in a savory blend of soy sauce, calamansi (or lemon) juice, and garlic. It is typically garnished with tender onion rings and served with steamed rice. The dish offers a perfect balance of salty, tangy, and savory flavors, making it a comforting and well-loved staple in Filipino households.

Dish Recipes:


  • 1 1/2 lbs Beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 5 tablespoons Soy sauce
  • 4 pieces Calamansi or 1-piece lemon
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 3 pieces Onion, sliced into rings
  • 4 tablespoons Cooking oil
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 pinch Salt



Instructions:


  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, calamansi, and ground black pepper for at least 1 hour. (Note: marinate overnight for best result)

  2. Heat the cooking oil in a pan then pan-fry half of the onions until the texture becomes soft. Set aside.

  3. Drain the marinade from the beef. Set it aside. Pan-fry the beef on the same pan where the onions were fried for 1 minute per side. Remove from the pan. Set aside.

  4. Add more oil if needed. Saute garlic and remaining raw onions until onion softens.

  5. Pour the remaining marinade and water. Bring to a boil.

  6. Add beef. Cover the pan and simmer until the meat is tender. (Note: Add water as needed)

  7. Season with ground black pepper and salt as needed. Top with pan-fried onions.

  8. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
