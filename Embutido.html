Embutido, or embotido, is a Philippine meatloaf made with ground pork and stuffed with hard-boiled eggs and sliced ham or various sausages. It is traditionally wrapped in aluminum foil and steamed, though it can also be baked.
=======
This Filipino-style meatloaf is made from ground pork mixed with vegetables, raisins, and sometimes hard-boiled eggs, then rolled into a log and steamed to perfection. The result is a savory, slightly sweet dish with a rich, tender texture. Typically served sliced, embutido is often enjoyed during festive occasions and is commonly paired with rice for a satisfying meal.
