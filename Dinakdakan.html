Dinakdakan, or warek-warek, is rooted in Ilocano cuisine from the Ilocos region in the Philippines. Its name comes from the Ilocano term “dakad,” meaning “to shake” or “to mix.” Traditionally, this refers to the method of mixing grilled pig parts with various seasonings and ingredients, which are vigorously tossed together.
=======
A flavorful Ilocano delicacy, this dish combines grilled and chopped pig parts—typically ears, face, and sometimes brain—for a smoky, chewy texture. It’s tossed in a creamy dressing made with vinegar, calamansi, onions, and chili, creating a bold, tangy, and spicy flavor profile. Often served as pulutan, it’s a standout for those who enjoy rich, savory bites with a kick.

Instructions:


  1. Pour 6 to 8 cups of water into a cooking pot. Let it boil.

  2. Once the water starts to boil, you have the option to add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Add in the pig ears and face. Set the heat to low and continue to boil for 50 to 60 minutes.

  3. Discard the water and let the excess water drip. Rub a little bit of salt all over the boiled ears and face. Rub the ginger powder on the liver.

  4. Heat up the grill. Grill the ears and face for 4 to 6 minutes per side or until it turns a bit crisp, but not burnt. Grill the liver for 5 to 8 minutes depending on thickness.

  5. Remove the grilled pig parts from the grill. Let them cool down and start chopping into bite-sized pieces.

  6. Meanwhile, combine mayonnaise and vinegar in a mixing bowl. Stir.

  7. Add some ground black pepper. Continue to stir until the ingredients are well blended.

  8. Add the ginger, chili, onion, and garlic powder (optional). Toss well to combine.
