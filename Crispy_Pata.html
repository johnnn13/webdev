Crispy Pata

Dish Description:

<<<<<<< HEAD
Crispy Pata or crispy pork leg is a popular Filipino pork dish. This dish can be eaten as a main dish along with rice and atcharang papaya. People also consume it as beer food or pulutan. It is best when dipped in a spicy vinegar mixture.
=======
Beloved Filipino dish made from deep-fried pork leg, known for its golden, crispy skin and tender, juicy meat inside. The pork leg is typically boiled until tender, then deep-fried to achieve its signature crunch. Served with a soy-vinegar dipping sauce with garlic and chili, Crispy Pata is a festive, indulgent dish often enjoyed during celebrations and special gatherings.

Instructions:


  1. Pour water into a cooking pot, then let it boil.

  2. Put in dried bay leaves, whole peppercorn, star anise, and 4 teaspoons of salt.

  3. Add the whole pig’s leg into the cooking pot, then simmer until the leg becomes tender (about 45 to 60 minutes).

  4. Remove the tender leg from the cooking pot and set aside until it cools down.

  5. Rub the leg with garlic powder, ground black pepper, and the remaining salt. Let it stand for 15 minutes to absorb the rub.

  6. Heat a clean large cooking pot (preferably with a cover) and pour in cooking oil.

  7. When the oil becomes hot, deep fry the rubbed pork leg. Continue cooking on medium heat until one side becomes crispy, then cautiously flip the leg to crisp the other side. Note: Be extra careful in doing this procedure.

  8. Turn off the heat, remove the crispy pork leg, and transfer it to a wide serving plate.

  9. Serve with atcharang papaya and soy sauce – vinegar dipping sauce.
