Hardinera

Dish Description:

A festive dish from Lucban, Quezon, this Filipino-style meatloaf is made with steamed pork mixed with eggs, bell peppers, raisins, pineapple, and hotdogs, creating a colorful and flavorful blend. Traditionally cooked in a llanera mold, the same used for leche flan, Hardinera is both savory and slightly sweet, often served during holidays and special occasions. Its vibrant presentation and rich taste make it a standout on any celebratory table.

Dish Recipes:


  • 2 tablespoons Cooking oil
  • 1 medium-sized Onion diced
  • 3 cloves Garlic minced
  • 1 lb Pork diced
  • 1 Cup Water
  • 1/2 cup Tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup Sweet pickle relish
  • 1/2 cup Beef franks or hotdogs diced
  • 1/4 cup Raisins
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground black pepper
  • 1/2 Beef cube
  • 1/2 cup Pineapple chunks
  • 1/4 cup Roasted sweet pepper
  • 1/4 cup Liver spread
  • 1/4 cup Cheese grated
  • 1/2 cup Bread crumbs
  • 2 Eggs hard-boiled, sliced, or wedged
  • 2 Raw eggs



Instructions:


  1. In a cooking pot, add cooking oil and sautee garlic and onions.

  2. Add the diced pork and cook for 3 to 5 minutes.

  3. Put-in the beef cube, water, and tomato sauce then bring to a boil. Simmer until the pork is tender and the liquid completely evaporates. Set aside and let cool.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked pork, salt, ground black pepper, beef franks, bread crumbs, liver spread, sweet pickle relish, pineapple chunks, raisins, and cheese.

  5. Mix all the ingredients until everything is well incorporated.

  6. Grease a llanera and arrange the sliced eggs and roasted sweet pepper.

  7. Pour close to half of the beaten egg mixture and ensure that the eggs are well distributed on the llanera.

  8. Scoop the pork mixture into the llanera until it occupies at least 3/4 of the total volume.

  9. Cover the whole llanera with aluminum foil.

  10. Pour at least 6 cups of water into the steamer. Arrange the containers in the steamer and steam for 50 minutes.

  11. Remove the steamed mixture from the llanera and transfer to a serving plate.

  12. Serve either hot or cold. Share and enjoy!
