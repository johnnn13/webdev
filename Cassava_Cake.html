Cassava Cake

Dish Description:

Cassava Cake is a classic Filipino dessert made from grated cassava (manioc). Cassava is also known as kamoteng kahoy and balinghoy in the Philippines. With Latin American roots, cassava was first introduced to the Philippines some time in the 16th century. Many also point out that this dish was a native of Lucban, Quezon.

Dish Recipes:


  • 2 lbs Cassava grated
  • 2 cups Coconut milk
  • 6 oz Evaporated milk
  • 3 pieces Egg
  • 1/4 cup Butter melted
  • 6 tablespoons Cheddar cheese grated
  • 1/2 cup Condensed milk
  • 14 tablespoons Granulated white sugar

Topping ingredients:


  • 2 tablespoons Flour
  • 2 tablespoons Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons Cheddar cheese grated
  • 2 cups Coconut milk


Instructions:


  1. Make the batter by combining the grated cassava, butter, 1/2 cup condensed milk, 6 oz. evaporated milk, 6 tablespoons cheddar cheese, 14 tablespoons sugar, and 2 eggs in a mixing bowl and mix thoroughly.

  2. Add the 2 cups of coconut milk to the mixing bowl where the mixed ingredients are. Mix again.

  3. Grease the baking tray then pour in the batter (these are the ingredients that you just mixed together).

  4. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 10 minutes then put in the baking tray with batter and bake for 1 hour. Remove from the oven and set aside.

  5. Meanwhile, prepare the topping by combining 2 tablespoons of sugar and flour in the heated saucepan.

  6. Pour in 1/2 cup condensed milk then mix thoroughly.

  7. Add 2 tablespoons cheddar cheese while stirring constantly.

  8. Pour 2 cups of coconut milk and stir constantly for 10 minutes.

  9. Pour the topping over the Cassava Cake (baked batter) and spread evenly.

  10. Separate the yolk from the egg white of the remaining egg (we'll be needing the egg white only).

  11. Glaze the topping with the egg white using a basting brush. Simply dip the brush into the egg white and brush it on the cassava cake.

  12. Set your oven to broil mode. Broil the Cassava cake until the color turns light brown.

  13. Garnish with extra grated cheese on top. Serve. Share and enjoy!
